I recently completed my journey with [Buildspace](https://buildspace.so) [Nights & Weekends](https://buildspace.so/nw) Season 5.
It was an incredible experience. But before diving into my personal journey, let me explain what Nights & Weekends is for those who might not be familiar.

## TLDR Lessons Learned

- **Commitment and Focus Matter**: The six-week structure of Nights & Weekends helped instill a sense of urgency and commitment. Having clear goals and regular updates kept the project on track and made the experience more rewarding.
- **Engage Early and Often**: Announcing a project before it's fully ready can build excitement and generate valuable feedback. This was a new approach for me, and it paid off in unexpected ways.
- **Embrace Uncertainty**: Attending an event with little information might be daunting, but it can lead to incredible experiences. The IRL event in San Francisco proved that stepping out of your comfort zone can result in significant personal and professional growth.
- **Community is Key**: The connections made with like-minded individuals who are passionate about their projects were invaluable. This sense of community provided support, inspiration, and a network of peers that I will cherish.
- **Seize Opportunities**: The unique experience of participating in Buildspace Nights & Weekends, especially with its final cohort, underscored the importance of taking advantage of opportunities when they arise. You never know when it might be the last chance.

## The Process

Nights & Weekends is a six-week program where you‚Äôre tasked with bringing your idea to life and focusing on a goal.
This structure was crucial for me. The weekly updates instilled a sense of urgency about my project.
Sharing my progress with others not only built excitement in them but also in me‚Äîa rare feeling for many of my projects!
This enthusiasm helped me stay committed to completing Nights & Weekends.

Each week, we had to submit our progress towards one of three goals we aimed to achieve.
For HireTrax, I set up a landing page and a mailing list for people to sign up.
I made posts on Threads and LinkedIn sharing my progress, and received some valuable feedback along the way.
The idea of announcing a project before it‚Äôs ready was new to me, but it actually worked‚Äîpeople engaged and got excited.

There was no agenda and little information about what we were supposed to do, which, as we learned on day one, was intentional.
Farza (the founder of Buildspace) gave us an amazing introduction, explaining that everyone participating in the IRL event was a bit ‚Äúweird.‚Äù
We had all accepted an invitation to San Francisco without knowing much of what we were in for.
